Chinese Street Food

Cifantuan or Ci Faan (Glutinous rice balls)

Rice balls filled with various flavorful local ingredients. The most common types are the savory kinds. These include aha cai (pickled vegetable), rousing (pork floss) and youtiao (long golden-brown strips of deep-fried dough). There are also the sweet variations, which consist of the same ingredients as the savory one but with added sugar as well as sesame.

Baozi (Chinese Bread Buns)

Dough buns often filled with juicy meat such as barbecued pork and/or vegetables, and steamed in steaming trays made of bamboo. Similar to Jiaozi, though they consist of a thicker dough, as well as a larger amount of filling.

Pai gu nian gao (Pork Chop with Rice Cakes)

Pork chop which is marinated and boiled which is then covered with thin rice flour and then fried.

Jianbing (Chinese Crepes)

A common food on the street eaten for breakfast. Basically found at every street corner, its main ingredients are wheat and grain flour then fried on a griddle with an egg. It's stuffed with scallions, lettuce, cilantro, and chili sauce.

Jiaozi (Chinese Dumplings)

Usually filled with veggies and/or meat, this tends to me a common street food aswell. They are shaped like ancient gold ingots, which were believed to bring you good luck. They are often shallow fried or deep-fried and eaten with a dip of soy-vinegar sauce for flavor. Also really popular on Chinese New Year.
